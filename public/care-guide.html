Garden Care Guide

Nurturing your digital garden

What is The Matrix Garden?

The Matrix is a living digital garden where ideas take root and grow. Each post-it note is like a seed—plant your thoughts, watch them flourish among others, and tend to the collective wisdom that blooms here.

Every four hours, the garden undergoes a transformation—a bloom cycle—where the space is reimagined, refreshed, and reborn based on the ideas planted by our community.

How to Tend Your Garden

Planting Ideas

  • Click the "Plant a New Idea" button to add a post-it note
  • Share your thoughts, questions, suggestions, or creative sparks
  • Each note requires a small proof-of-work—a gentle commitment to quality over quantity
  • Your note will bloom into the garden with a unique color and position

Arranging the Garden

  • Drag and drop any note to rearrange the garden layout
  • Create visual patterns, group related ideas, or simply make space
  • The garden is collaborative—everyone helps shape its form

Tending Your Own

  • Click on your own notes to edit the text
  • Remove notes you've planted if they no longer serve the garden
  • Each gardener is responsible for their own contributions

Gardener's Tip: Notes are read-only for everyone except their creator. This protects the integrity of each voice while allowing collaborative arrangement of the space.

The Bloom Cycles

Every four hours, the garden undergoes a transformation. The most-voted suggestions from the previous cycle guide the aesthetic and functional changes to the site. This is how the garden stays alive, evolving with each season.

🌱 Spring

New growth, fresh beginnings

🌸 Summer

Full bloom, abundant life

🍂 Autumn

Harvest, reflection

❄️ Winter

Rest, preparation

When a new cycle begins, all notes are cleared—composted, if you will—to make room for fresh growth. The previous cycle's ideas are archived and viewable, ensuring nothing is truly lost.

Gardener's Tip: Plant your most meaningful ideas early in each cycle. They have more time to gather attention and influence the next transformation.

Best Practices

  • Be thoughtful: Each idea you plant contributes to the collective ecosystem
  • Be concise: Clear, focused thoughts bloom better than rambling prose
  • Be respectful: This is a shared garden—tend it with care for all who visit
  • Be creative: Unusual ideas, wild suggestions, and playful thoughts make the garden interesting
  • Be patient: Good gardens take time. Let ideas develop naturally

Technical Details

Proof of Work

To prevent spam and encourage thoughtful contributions, each note requires solving a small computational puzzle. This happens automatically in your browser—you'll see a brief "growing..." message while the work completes.

Privacy & Ownership

Notes are identified by a unique author label generated from your browser's fingerprint. You maintain ownership of your notes for editing and deletion, but all contributions are public to the garden.

Garden Architecture

The site is built with vanilla HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Each cycle transformation is performed by an autonomous agent that reads community suggestions and reimagines the garden's aesthetic and features. The core functionality—planting, arranging, and tending notes—remains constant across all seasons.

Questions?

If you're curious about how the garden evolves, check the Garden Log to see the history of transformations. Each entry shows what changed and why.

The garden is an experiment in collaborative creativity, algorithmic stewardship, and organic digital spaces. There are no strict rules—just gentle guidance and the wisdom of the community.

Remember: Every great garden started with a single seed. Your contribution matters. 🌱
